## PatternFly 6 Overview

PatternFly is Red Hat's open source design system. It consists of components, documentation, and code for building enterprise applications at scale.

## Design Kit and Getting Started

To start designing with PatternFly, you will need to install the PatternFly 6 design kit. This kit gives you access to PatternFly's visual design system — including design tokens — so that you can create consistent and usable product experiences.(no content)PatternFly 6 is built off of a new design token system. ## Layout Components and Patterns

PatternFly 6: Patterns & Extensions Library provides several patterns and demos that solve common UI problems by grouping multiple PatternFly components into reusable layouts. PatternFly 6: Design Tokens & Styles contains all the variables for color, spacing and typography that convey the associated design style for PatternFly.(no content)The PatternFly Design Kit is a collection of Figma components that make it easy for designers to create low and high-fidelity design mockups that accurately represent the PatternFly components and layouts.

## Best Practices

Nothing created in a silo makes its way to PatternFly. They believe design is better in the open. This is why they include the community in all updates, changes, and additions and collect feedback on contributions from people across design and development.(no content)They have regular design meetings where contributors present their ideas and discuss design approaches in a group setting. This enables them to keep ideas collaborative and consider designs from all angles.(no content)As a community, they aim to have all PatternFly contributions lead to functional and beautiful product experiences across different contexts. They work with UX researchers to test updates, changes, and additions with users to ensure they're creating designs that solve for everyone.
