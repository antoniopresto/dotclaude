   451→### PERF-002: Viewport Culling & Spatial Indexing - PLANNED
   452→### BUG-009: Trackpad Zoom Performance - IDENTIFIED
   453→
   454→**Objective:**
   455→Deep research and planning for viewport culling optimization + Investigation of trackpad zoom performance issue on MacBook.
   456→
   457→**Research Conducted:**
   458→- Web searches: Three.js frustum culling, R3F viewport culling, RBush spatial indexing, deck.gl massive rendering, Figma WebGL architecture
   459→- Read: `docs/state-management-research/relatorio-state-management.pdf` (11 pages)
   460→- Read: `docs/RESEARCH.md` (railway scheduling - not relevant)
   461→- Read: `apps/canvas/src/canvas/InfiniteCanvas.tsx` (current LOD implementation)
   462→
   463→**Key Findings:**
   464→
   465→1. **InstancedMesh Problem:** Three.js sends ALL instances to GPU regardless of viewport
   466→   - 60k instances = 60k transforms uploaded every frame
   467→   - No built-in per-instance frustum culling
   468→
   469→2. **RBush Spatial Index:**
   470→   - Bulk load 1M items ~1600ms (one-time)
   471→   - Viewport query for 1000 results ~4ms
   472→   - Perfect for viewport culling queries
   473→
   474→3. **Culling Strategy:**
   475→   - Reorder visible instances to front of buffer
   476→   - Set `mesh.count = visibleCount` (only visible rendered)
   477→   - No new allocations during pan/zoom
   478→
   479→4. **Alternative Libraries:**
   480→   - `@three.ez/instanced-mesh` (InstancedMesh2) - has BVH + per-instance culling
   481→   - `three-mesh-bvh` - accelerated raycasting
   482→   - `flatbush` - 5-8x faster than RBush for static data
   483→
   484→**Documents Created:**
   485→- `docs/VIEWPORT_CULLING_RESEARCH.md` - Comprehensive research consolidating all findings
   486→
   487→**Task Created: PERF-002**
   488→
