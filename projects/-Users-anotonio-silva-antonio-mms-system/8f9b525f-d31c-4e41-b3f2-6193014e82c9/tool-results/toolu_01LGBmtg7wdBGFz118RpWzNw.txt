## Origin

The Ralph Wiggum Technique, created by Geoff Huntley, went viral in the final weeks of 2025.(no content)The story begins in roughly May 2025 with Geoffrey Huntley, a longtime open source software developer who pivoted to raising goats in rural Australia.

## The Technique

Ralph is a technique. In its purest form, Ralph is a Bash loop. As Huntley describes it: `while :; do cat PROMPT.md | claude-code ; done`

The technique is named after Ralph Wiggum from The Simpsons, embodying the philosophy of persistent iteration despite setbacks.(no content)The name comes from Ralph Wiggum of The Simpsons - perpetually confused, always making mistakes, but never stopping.

## Why "Ralph"?

Interestingly, when Huntley discovered this in February, it literally made him want to "Ralph." Ralph is a term for vomiting. And it wasn't named initially, but it made him want to vomit because he could actually see where we're going.

## The Philosophy

Horthy and Huntley argue that the power of the original Ralph wasn't just in the looping, but in its "naive persistence" — the unsanitized feedback, in which the LLM isn't protected from its own mess; it is forced to confront it. It embodies the philosophy that if you press the model hard enough against its own failures without a safety net, it will eventually "dream" a correct solution just to escape the loop.

## Official Adoption by Anthropic

By late 2025, Boris Cherny, Anthropic's Head of Claude Code, formalized the hack into the official ralph-wiggum plugin.(no content)Implementation of the Ralph Wiggum technique for iterative, self-referential AI development loops in Claude Code. Ralph is a development methodology based on continuous AI agent loops. As Geoffrey Huntley describes it: "Ralph is a Bash loop" - a simple while true that repeatedly feeds an AI agent a prompt file, allowing it to iteratively improve its work until completion.

## Community Reception

Some users were disappointed with the official plugin. It dies in cryptic ways unless you have --dangerously-skip-permissions. It installs hooks in weird places, uses a strange markdown file to track state. Beyond that, it misses the key point of ralph which is not "run forever" but in "carve off small bits of work into independent context windows".(no content)However, as noted by critics, the official release marked a shift in philosophy—a "sterilization" of the original chaotic concept. While Huntley's script was about brute force, the official Anthropic plugin was designed around the principle that "Failures Are Data."
