Railway Maintenance Domain\n\n### 1.1 ECM Framework (EU 2019/779)\n\nThe **Entity in Charge of Maintenance (ECM)** framework establishes legal responsibilities for railway vehicle maintenance. While Dubai Metro is outside EU jurisdiction, the ECM framework represents industry best practice.\n\n#### ECM Function Mapping\n\n| Function | Responsibility | MMS V1.0 Feature | Status |\n|----------|----------------|------------------|--------|\n| **ECM-F1** | Maintenance Management | Dashboard KPIs, compliance tracking | Visualization only |\n| **ECM-F2** | Maintenance Development | Interval definitions, plan visualization | Mock data display |\n| **ECM-F3** | Fleet Maintenance Management | Real-time fleet status, conflict detection | Conflict indicators |\n| **ECM-F4** | Maintenance Delivery | Work order visualization, depot allocation | Visual representation |\n\n#### ECM Compliance Requirements\n\n| Requirement | ECM Article | MMS V1.0 Implementation |\n|-------------|-------------|------------------------|\n| Risk assessment | Art. 5.2 | Conflict detection, overdue warnings |\n| Competence management | Art. 5.3 | Not implemented |\n| Documentation | Art. 5.4 | Not implemented |\n| Performance monitoring | Art. 5.5 | KPI display |\n\n---\n\n### 1.2 RAMS Compliance (EN 50126-1)\n\n**RAMS** = Reliability, Availability, Maintainability, Safety\n\nEN 50126-1:2017 defines the railway RAMS lifecycle. MMS visualizes RAMS metrics.\n\n#### RAMS Metric Definitions\n\n| Aspect | Definition | MMS V1.0 Feature |\n|--------|------------|------------------|\n| **Reliability** | Probability of intended function under stated conditions | MTBF display |\n| **Availability** | Ability to perform required function at given instant | Fleet availability % |\n| **Maintainability** | Probability of maintenance within stated time | MTTR display |\n| **Safety** | Freedom from unacceptable risk | Critical task priority |\n\n#### RAMS Targets\n\n| Metric | Formula | Target | Critical Threshold |\n|--------|---------|--------|-------------------|\n| Fleet Availability | (Fit Trains / Total Trains) x 100 | >= 97% | < 95% |\n| MTBF | Total Operating Hours / Number of Failures | Operator-defined | Operator-defined |\n| MTTR | Total Downtime Hours / Number of Repairs | <= 24 hours | > 48 hours |\n| Schedule Adherence | (On-Time / Scheduled) x 100 | >= 95% | < 90% |\n\n---\n\n### 1.3 EN 15380 Railway Terminology\n\nAll user-facing labels MUST use EN 15380 compliant terminology.\n\n#### Mandatory Term Usage\n\n| EN 15380 Term | Definition | NOT Acceptable |\n|---------------|------------|----------------|\n| Rolling Stock | Railway vehicles requiring maintenance | Vehicle, Asset, Unit |\n| Fleet | Group of rolling stock sharing manufacturer/model | Group, Class, Collection |\n| Depot | Designated maintenance facility | Garage, Workshop, Station |\n| Train Fitness | Binary operational readiness (fit/unfit) | Availability, Health, Status |\n| Run Assignment | Daily allocation of train IDs to routes | Dispatch, Schedule, Trip |\n| Dead Running | Non-revenue movement between locations | Empty movement, Repositioning |\n| Mileage Balancing | Strategy to equalize wear across fleet | Usage balancing |\n\n#### Maintenance Terminology\n\n| Term | Definition | Context |\n|------|------------|---------|\n| PM (Preventive Maintenance) | Scheduled maintenance at defined intervals | Time or mileage-based |\n| CM (Corrective Maintenance) | Unscheduled repair after failure detection | Reactive |\n| CBM (Condition-Based Maintenance) | Triggered by condition monitoring | Sensor-based |\n| Overhaul | Major refurbishment restoring to original condition | Resets counters |\n| Heavy Maintenance | Significant maintenance requiring extended depot stay | Multi-day duration |\n\n---\n\n## 2.
