   344→## Chapter 1: Executive Summary
   345→
   346→### 1.1 Project Overview
   347→
   348→**MMS (Metro Mileage Management System)** is an enterprise solution designed to centralize and automate mileage management for mixed rolling stock fleets exceeding 100 units. The system bridges the gap between Operations and Maintenance departments through predictive, data-driven planning.
   349→
   350→**Core Problem Statement:**
   351→
   352→Railway operators managing large fleets face three critical pain points:
   353→
   354→1. **Over-running trains** - Units exceeding target mileage degrade faster, increasing maintenance costs and reducing asset lifespan
   355→2. **Under-running trains** - Units falling below target mileage represent idle capital with poor return on investment
   356→3. **Manual planning limitations** - Excel-based planning does not scale for 100+ train fleets and cannot optimize across multiple constraints
   357→
   358→**Solution Vision:**
   359→
   360→MMS addresses all three pain points through:
   361→- Intelligent mileage balancing across the entire fleet
   362→- AI-powered train recommendations with visible justifications
   363→- Predictive maintenance scheduling coordinated with operations
   364→- What-if simulation for strategic planning
   365→
   366→### 1.2 Business Objective
   367→
   368→**Primary Goal:** Win a competitive bid for a railway fleet management contract through a compelling 15-minute demo presentation.
   369→
   370→**Target Audience:**
   371→- Operations Director (decision maker)
   372→- Client's CTO (technical validator)
   373→- Maintenance leadership (domain experts)
   374→
   375→**Success Statement:**

   455→## Chapter 2: Railway Domain Fundamentals
   456→
   457→### 2.1 Essential Glossary
   458→
   459→All UI labels and system messages MUST use standard EN 15380 railway terminology. Using incorrect terms destroys credibility with railway professionals.
   460→
   461→**Core Terms:**
   462→
   463→| Term | Definition | NOT Acceptable |
   464→|------|------------|----------------|
   465→| Rolling Stock | Railway vehicles requiring maintenance (trains, carriages, locomotives) | Vehicle, Asset, Unit |
   466→| Fleet | Group of rolling stock sharing the same manufacturer/model and maintenance profile | Group, Class, Collection |
   467→| Depot | Designated maintenance facility for a fleet | Garage, Workshop, Station |
   468→| Train Fitness | Binary operational readiness status (fit/unfit) | Availability, Health, Status |
   469→| Run Assignment | Daily allocation of train IDs to routes/services | Dispatch, Schedule, Trip |
   470→| Dead Running | Non-revenue movement of train between locations | Empty movement, Repositioning |
   471→| Service | A scheduled revenue-generating journey from origin to destination | Route, Trip, Run |
   472→| Mileage Balancing | Strategy to equalize wear across fleet by controlling km distribution | Usage balancing, Wear distribution |
   473→
   474→**Maintenance Terms:**
   475→
   476→| Term | Definition | Typical Interval |
   477→|------|------------|------------------|
   478→| PM1 | Preventive Maintenance Level 1 (light inspection) | Every 15,000 km |
   479→| PM2 | Preventive Maintenance Level 2 (medium service) | Every 45,000 km |
   480→| HM | Heavy Maintenance (major overhaul) | Every 90,000 km |
   481→| OVH | Overhaul (complete refurbishment) | Every 6 years or 500,000 km |
   482→
   483→**Operational Terms:**
   484→
   485→| Term | Definition |
   486→|------|------------|
   487→| TCO | Total Cost of Ownership over fleet lifecycle |
   488→| Overrun | Train that has exceeded its target mileage |
   489→| Underrun | Train that falls short of its target mileage |
   490→| Variance | Statistical measure of mileage distribution spread across fleet |
   491→| Aptitude | Train's certification for specific line/speed requirements |
   492→
   493→### 2.2 Business Entities & Relationships
   494→
   495→**Entity Relationship Model:**
   496→
   497→```
   498→┌─────────────┐         ┌─────────────┐
   499→│    Fleet    │────────│    Train    │
   500→│             │   1:N  │             │
   501→│ - name      │        │ - train_id  │
   502→│ - pm1_km    │        │ - current_km│
   503→│ - pm2_km    │        │ - target_km │
   504→│ - hm_km     │        │ - fitness   │
   505→└─────────────┘        └──────┬──────┘
   506→                              │
   507→       ┌──────────────────────┼──────────────────────┐
   508→       │                      │                      │
   509→       ▼                      ▼                      ▼
   510→┌─────────────┐        ┌─────────────┐        ┌─────────────┐
   511→│    Depot    │        │ Allocation  │        │ Maintenance │
   512→│             │        │             │        │  Schedule   │
   513→│ - name      │        │ - date      │        │             │
   514→│ - capacity  │        │ - status    │        │ - type      │
   515→│ - manhours  │        │ - score     │        │ - date      │
   516→└─────────────┘        │ - reasons   │        │ - depot     │
   517→                       └──────┬──────┘        └─────────────┘
   518→                              │
   519→                              ▼
   520→                       ┌─────────────┐
   521→                       │   Service   │
   522→                       │             │
   523→                       │ - code      │
   524→                       │ - origin    │
   525→                       │ - dest      │
   526→                       │ - distance  │
   527→                       └─────────────┘
   528→```
   529→
   530→**Cardinality Rules:**
   531→
   532→- One Fleet has many Trains (1:N)
   533→- One Train belongs to one Fleet (N:1)
   534→- One Depot can host trains from multiple Fleets (N:M)
   535→- One Train has one Base Depot (N:1)
   536→- One Service can have many Allocations (different dates) (1:N)
   537→- One Train can have many Allocations (different services) (1:N)
   538→- One Train can have many Maintenance Schedules (1:N)
   539→
   540→### 2.3 Train Lifecycle & Maintenance Hierarchy
   541→
   542→**Maintenance Cascade:**
   543→
   544→```
   545→Fleet Type (e.g., Metro Series 3400)
   546→ └─ Maintenance Profile
   547→     ├─ PM1 (every 15,000 km)
   548→     │   └─ Duration: 1 day
   549→     │   └─ Tasks: Inspection, fluid top-up, brake check
   550→     │
   551→     ├─ PM2 (every 45,000 km)
   552→     │   └─ Duration: 3 days
   553→     │   └─ Tasks: Component replacement, deep inspection
   554→     │   └─ Note: Includes PM1 tasks
   555→     │
   556→     ├─ HM (every 90,000 km)
   557→     │   └─ Duration: 7-14 days
   558→     │   └─ Tasks: Major component overhaul, bogie work
   559→     │   └─ Note: Includes PM1 + PM2 tasks
   560→     │
   561→     └─ OVH (every 6 years or 500,000 km)
   562→         └─ Duration: 30-60 days
   563→         └─ Tasks: Complete refurbishment
   564→         └─ Note: Resets maintenance counters
   565→```
   566→
   567→**Train State Machine:**
   568→
   569→```
   570→    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────┐
   571→    │                                             │
   572→    ▼                                             │
   573→┌───────┐     ┌────────────┐     ┌────────────┐  │
   574→│  FIT  │────▶│ IN-SERVICE │────▶│ AVAILABLE  │──┘
   575→│       │     │            │     │            │
   576→└───┬───┘     └────────────┘     └─────┬──────┘
   577→    │                                  │
   578→    │         ┌────────────┐           │
   579→    └────────▶│   UNFIT    │◀──────────┘
   580→              │            │
   581→              └─────┬──────┘
   582→                    │
   583→                    ▼
   584→              ┌────────────┐
   585→              │MAINTENANCE │
   586→              │            │
   587→              └─────┬──────┘
   588→                    │
   589→                    ▼
   590→              ┌────────────┐
   591→              │    FIT     │
   592→              └────────────┘
   593→```
   594→
   595→### 2.4 Critical Domain Rules (Unknown Unknowns)
   596→
   597→These are domain constraints that seasoned railway professionals know intuitively but are rarely documented. Violating these rules destroys credibility instantly.
   598→
   599→**Hard Constraints (Never Violate):**
   600→
   601→1. **Unfit trains are NEVER assigned to service** - No exception, no override
   602→2. **Maintenance windows are sacred** - A train scheduled for PM cannot be reassigned
   603→3. **Aptitude requirements are binary** - A train either can or cannot run on a line
   604→4. **Depot capacity is physical** - You cannot exceed track slots, period
   605→5. **Service cannot be cancelled to balance km** - Operations > Optimization
   606→
   607→**Soft Constraints (Optimize Within):**
   608→
   609→1. **Km balancing priority** - Under-running trains should get high-km services
   610→2. **Dead running minimization** - Prefer trains that end near next service start
   611→3. **Maintenance depot proximity** - Schedule services that end near maintenance depot
   612→4. **Crew familiarity** - Same crews prefer same train types (not modeled in MVP)
   613→
   614→**Critical Timing Rules:**
   615→
   616→| Rule | Specification |
   617→|------|---------------|
   618→| PM1 tolerance window | Can be ±500 km from trigger |
   619→| PM2 tolerance window | Can be ±1,000 km from trigger |
   620→| HM tolerance window | Can be ±2,000 km from trigger |
   621→| Overrun alert | Train at 95% of next PM triggers warning |
   622→| Critical overrun | Train at 105% of next PM triggers alert |
