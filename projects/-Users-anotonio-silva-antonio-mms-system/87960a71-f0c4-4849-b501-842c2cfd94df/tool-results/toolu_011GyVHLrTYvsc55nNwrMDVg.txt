     6→## MAINTENANCE TERMS
     7→
     8→### PM (Preventive Maintenance)
     9→**English:** Preventive Maintenance  
    10→**Português:** Manutenção Preventiva
    11→
    12→Scheduled maintenance performed BEFORE equipment failure, based on mileage intervals or time. The goal is to PREVENT breakdowns, not fix them after they happen.
    13→
    14→---
    15→
    16→### PM50k (Preventive Maintenance at 50,000 km)
    17→**English:** Preventive Maintenance at 50,000 km interval  
    18→**Português:** Manutenção Preventiva no intervalo de 50.000 km
    19→
    20→Basic preventive maintenance performed when a train reaches 50,000 km since its last PM50k. 
    21→
    22→**Scope includes:**
    23→- Basic inspection
    24→- Filter replacement
    25→- Routine checks
    26→- Fluid top-ups
    27→
    28→**Typical duration:** 1-2 days
    29→
    30→---
    31→
    32→### PM100k (Preventive Maintenance at 100,000 km)
    33→**English:** Preventive Maintenance at 100,000 km interval  
    34→**Português:** Manutenção Preventiva no intervalo de 100.000 km
    35→
    36→More comprehensive maintenance performed every 100,000 km.
    37→
    38→**Scope includes:**
    39→- Everything in PM50k
    40→- Wear item replacement (brake pads, etc.)
    41→- Critical component inspection
    42→- Electrical system checks
    43→
    44→**Typical duration:** 3-5 days
    45→
    46→---
    47→
    48→### PM150k (Preventive Maintenance at 150,000 km)
    49→**English:** Preventive Maintenance at 150,000 km interval  
    50→**Português:** Manutenção Preventiva no intervalo de 150.000 km
    51→
    52→Major revision focusing on critical components and safety systems.
    53→
    54→**Scope includes:**
    55→- Everything in PM100k
    56→- Safety system overhaul
    57→- Major component inspection
    58→- Structural checks
    59→
    60→**Typical duration:** 5-7 days
    61→
    62→---
    63→
    64→### Overhaul (Major Overhaul / General Revision)
    65→**English:** Major Overhaul / General Revision  
    66→**Português:** Revisão Geral / Overhaul
    67→
    68→Complete general revision of the entire train. The most extensive and expensive maintenance type.
    69→
    70→**Scope includes:**
    71→- Complete reconditioning
    72→- Major component replacement
    73→- Repainting
    74→- Interior refurbishment
    75→- Full system testing
    76→
    77→**Typical duration:** 10-15 days
    78→
    79→---
    80→
    81→### Man-hours
    82→**English:** Man-hours (unit of labor)  
    83→**Português:** Homens-hora (unidade de trabalho)
    84→
    85→Unit of labor measurement. If a task requires 100 man-hours, it can be completed by:
    86→- 1 person in 100 hours, OR
    87→- 10 people in 10 hours, OR
    88→- 20 people in 5 hours
    89→
    90→Used for resource planning and workload leveling.
    91→

    94→## SYSTEM CONCEPTS
    95→
    96→### RUC (Recommended Usage Category)
    97→**English:** Recommended Usage Category  
    98→**Português:** Categoria de Uso Recomendada
    99→
   100→Daily classification assigned to each train indicating how it should be used to keep mileage aligned with the Master Plan.
   101→
   102→**Three categories:**
   103→
   104→| Category | Meaning | Action |
   105→|----------|---------|--------|
   106→| ACCELERATE | Train is BEHIND plan (negative drift) | Use more: longer routes, peak hours |
   107→| NOMINAL | Train is ON TRACK (drift ≈ 0) | Normal operation |
   108→| DECELERATE | Train is AHEAD of plan (positive drift) | Use less: shorter routes, use as spare |
   109→
   110→---
   111→
   112→### Drift
   113→**English:** Drift / Deviation  
   114→**Português:** Desvio
   115→
   116→The difference between a train's ACTUAL mileage and its PLANNED mileage at any given moment.
   117→
   118→**Formula:** `Drift = Actual_km - Planned_km`
   119→
   120→| Drift | Meaning | RUC Assignment |
   121→|-------|---------|----------------|
   122→| Positive (+) | Train is AHEAD of plan | DECELERATE |
   123→| Zero (0) | Train is ON TRACK | NOMINAL |
   124→| Negative (-) | Train is BEHIND plan | ACCELERATE |
   125→
   126→---
   127→
   128→### Master Plan
   129→**English:** Master Plan / Optimized Long-term Schedule  
   130→**Português:** Plano Mestre / Cronograma Otimizado de Longo Prazo
   131→
   132→The optimized maintenance schedule for the entire fleet (128 trains) covering the next 12 years. 
   133→
   134→**Contains for each maintenance event:**
   135→- Scheduled date
   136→- Train ID
   137→- Maintenance type (PM50k, PM100k, etc.)
   138→- Expected km at maintenance start
   139→- Required resources (tracks, man-hours)
   140→
   141→This is the PRIMARY OUTPUT of the Strategic Layer.
   142→
   143→---
   144→
   145→### Workload Leveling
   146→**English:** Workload Leveling / Resource Leveling  
   147→**Português:** Nivelamento de Carga de Trabalho
   148→
   149→The practice of distributing work evenly over time to avoid:
   150→- **Peaks:** Weeks where demand exceeds capacity (requires overtime, delays)
   151→- **Valleys:** Weeks with little work (idle resources, wasted capacity)
   152→
   153→**Benefits:**
   154→- Predictable resource needs
   155→- No overtime costs
   156→- Efficient utilization
   157→- Better budgeting
   158→

   161→## TECHNICAL TERMS
   162→
   163→### MIP (Mixed-Integer Programming)
   164→**English:** Mixed-Integer Programming  
   165→**Português:** Programação Inteira Mista
   166→
   167→Mathematical optimization technique that combines:
   168→- **Continuous variables:** Any real number (e.g., 3.14, 100.5)
   169→- **Integer variables:** Whole numbers only, often binary 0/1 (e.g., "yes/no" decisions)
   170→
   171→**Used by:**
   172→- Airlines (crew scheduling)
   173→- Logistics (vehicle routing)
   174→- Manufacturing (production planning)
   175→- Our system (maintenance scheduling)
   176→
   177→---
   178→
   179→### Solver
   180→**English:** Optimization Solver  
   181→**Português:** Solucionador de Otimização
   182→
   183→Software that finds solutions to mathematical optimization problems.
   184→
   185→**Input:**
   186→- Decision variables (what to decide)
   187→- Constraints (rules to follow)
   188→- Objective function (what to optimize)
   189→
   190→**Output:**
   191→- Optimal solution (best possible answer)
   192→- Or proof that no solution exists
   193→
   194→**Examples:** HiGHS, Gurobi, CPLEX, OR-Tools
   195→
   196→---
   197→
   198→### Constraint
   199→**English:** Constraint / Restriction  
   200→**Português:** Restrição
   201→
   202→A rule that any valid solution MUST satisfy.
   203→
   204→**Examples in our system:**
   205→- "Maximum 4 trains in maintenance per day" (track capacity)
   206→- "PM100k can only happen AFTER PM50k is complete" (precedence)
   207→- "Maintenance must occur within ±15 days of target date" (flexibility window)
   208→- "Weekly man-hours cannot exceed 800" (labor capacity)
   209→
   210→---
   211→
   212→### Objective Function
   213→**English:** Objective Function  
   214→**Português:** Função Objetivo
   215→
   216→The mathematical expression to be minimized or maximized.
   217→
   218→**Our objective:**
   219→```
   220→Minimize σ² (variance of weekly man-hours)
   221→```
   222→
   223→This means: make the workload as EVEN as possible across all weeks.
   224→

   228→**English:** High-performance Interior-point Gaussian elimination Solver  
   229→**Português:** Solver de alto desempenho HiGHS
   230→
   231→Open-source optimization solver we use in this project.
   232→

   237→- Development: Active, maintained by Edinburgh University

   275→## FORMULAS
   276→
   277→### Drift Calculation
   278→```
   279→Drift = Actual_km - Planned_km
   280→Drift_percentage = (Drift / Planned_km) × 100%
   281→```
   282→
   283→### RUC Thresholds (configurable)
   284→```
   285→If Drift < -5%  → ACCELERATE
   286→If Drift > +5%  → DECELERATE
   287→Else            → NOMINAL
   288→```
   289→
   290→### Days Until Maintenance
   291→```
   292→Days_remaining = (PM_threshold - Current_km) / Avg_daily_km
   293→```
