    23→## Executive Summary
    24→
    25→**MMS (Metro Mileage Management System)** is an enterprise solution to centralize and automate mileage management for mixed rolling stock fleets (>100 units), bridging Operations and Maintenance via predictive, data-driven planning.
    26→
    27→### Vision
    28→
    29→Create a functional demo in **3 months** that:
    30→- Demonstrates technical competence in railway fleet management
    31→- Proves deep understanding of the railway domain
    32→- Impresses decision-makers (Operations Director, client's CTO)
    33→
    34→### Success Metric
    35→
    36→> After 15 minutes of demo, the client thinks: **"They understand our problem better than the others."**
    37→
    38→### Core Problem
    39→
    40→Railway operators face three critical pain points:
    41→1. **Over-running trains** degrade faster, increasing maintenance cost
    42→2. **Under-running trains** represent idle capital
    43→3. **Manual planning in Excel** doesn't scale for 100+ train fleets
    44→
    45→MMS addresses all three points through intelligent mileage balancing, maintenance scheduling, and operations coordination.
    46→
    47→### What Makes This Special
    48→
    49→| Differentiator | Why It Matters |
    50→|----------------|----------------|
    51→| **Km balancing as primary objective** | Competitors treat as secondary feature |
    52→| **Unified Maintenance ↔ Operations interface** | Eliminates silos, reduces rework |
    53→| **Visible dead running cost** | Hidden cost made explicit and optimized |
    54→| **Accessible what-if simulation** | Decision-makers can "play" with scenarios |
    55→| **Visible decision justification** | Trust, not black box - every recommendation explains why |

   369→## Domain Requirements
   370→
   371→### Railway Domain Terminology
   372→
   373→All UI labels and messages MUST use standard EN 15380 railway terminology:
   374→
   375→| UI Label | Domain Term | NOT Acceptable |
   376→|----------|-------------|----------------|
   377→| Rolling Stock | Rolling Stock | Vehicle, Train, Asset |
   378→| Fleet | Fleet | Group, Class |
   379→| Depot | Depot | Garage, Workshop |
   380→| Preventive Maintenance | PM1, PM2, HM | Light/Heavy Service |
   381→| Train Fitness | Fitness | Availability |
   382→| Run Assignment | Allocation | Dispatch |
   383→| Dead Running | Dead Running | Empty Movement |
   384→| Service | Service | Route, Trip |
   385→
   386→### Maintenance Hierarchy
   387→
   388→```
   389→Fleet Type
   390→ └─ Maintenance Profile
   391→     ├─ PM1 (every 15,000 km) - Light preventive
   392→     ├─ PM2 (every 45,000 km) - Medium preventive
   393→     ├─ HM (every 90,000 km) - Heavy overhaul
   394→     └─ Overhaul (every 6 years) - Full refurbishment
   395→```
   396→
   397→### Constraint Priority (Solver Must Respect)
   398→
   399→1. **Train Fitness** - Unfit trains never assigned (hard constraint)
   400→2. **Maintenance Windows** - Scheduled maintenance blocks availability
   401→3. **Resource Limits** - Man-hours, track slots per depot
   402→4. **Km Balancing** - Optimize within above constraints
   403→
   404→### Entity Relationships
   405→
   406→```
   407→Fleet (1) ---- (*) Train
   408→  |                 |
   409→  |                 +---- (*) RunAssignment
   410→  |                 |
   411→  |                 +---- (1) TrainFitness [daily state]
   412→  |
   413→  +---- (1) BaseDepot
   414→  |
   415→  +---- (1) MaintenanceProfile
   416→```

   704→## Appendix: Domain Glossary
   705→
   706→| Term | Definition |
   707→|------|------------|
   708→| Rolling Stock | Railway vehicles (trains, carriages) requiring maintenance |
   709→| Fleet | Group of rolling stock sharing same manufacturer/model and maintenance profile |
   710→| Base Depot | Designated maintenance facility for a fleet |
   711→| Train Fitness | Binary operational readiness (fit/unfit) |
   712→| Run Assignment | Daily allocation of train IDs to routes |
   713→| Mileage Balancing | Strategy to equalize wear across fleet |
   714→| Dead Running | Non-revenue movement of train between locations |
   715→| PM1/PM2/HM | Preventive Maintenance levels (light/medium/heavy) |
   716→| EAM | Enterprise Asset Management - external system |
   717→| TCO | Total Cost of Ownership over fleet lifecycle |
   718→| Overrun | Train exceeds target mileage |
   719→| Underrun | Train falls short of target mileage |
   720→| Variance | Measure of mileage distribution spread across fleet |
