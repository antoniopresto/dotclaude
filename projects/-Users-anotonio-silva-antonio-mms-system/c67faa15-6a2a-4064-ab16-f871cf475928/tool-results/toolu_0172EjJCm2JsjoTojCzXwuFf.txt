    10→## 1.1 System Purpose
    11→
    12→The Metro Fleet Mileage Management System (MMS) is a constraint-based fleet management platform that optimizes train deployments and maintenance scheduling over a 12-year contract period. It replaces manual Excel-based planning with an integrated system providing:
    13→
    14→1. **Daily mileage tracking** with automated ingestion from CSV uploads
    15→2. **Run assignment optimization** balancing mileage across the fleet
    16→3. **Preventive maintenance scheduling** within resource constraints
    17→4. **Strategic planning** for 1-10 year horizons to prevent "bow wave" accumulation
    18→5. **Man-hour leveling** to optimize workforce utilization
    19→6. **Cost forecasting** with TCO projection and variance analysis
    20→7. **Simulation capabilities** for what-if scenario planning
    21→8. **Unified visualization** via an Infinite Canvas interface

    25→## 1.2 Key Architectural Decisions
    26→
    27→| ID | Decision | Rationale | Alternatives Considered | Risk Mitigation |
    28→|----|----------|-----------|------------------------|-----------------|
    29→| ADR-001 | TypeScript-First Architecture | Team expertise, single language for 98% of codebase, strong typing | Java (enterprise), Python (data science) | Node.js fallback path documented |
    30→| ADR-002 | C++ Native Binary for Tactical Optimization | Container density (20-30 pods/node), <5ms cold start, OR-Tools native performance | WASM solver (limited), Pure TS (10x slower) | TypeScript fallback solver, Docker build pipeline |
    31→| ADR-003 | Dual-Horizon Planning (Strategic + Tactical) | RFQ requires 1-10 year planning; single solver cannot handle both granularities | Single solver (bow wave risk), Manual strategic planning | Constraint aggregation layer ensures consistency |
    32→| ADR-004 | Soft Constraints with Explainability | Operations cannot accept "INFEASIBLE"; must provide actionable least-bad solutions | Hard constraints only (operationally unacceptable) | Fallback greedy solver, explanation trace |
    33→| ADR-005 | Material Constraints in Optimizer | PM scheduling without parts availability is operationally useless | Labor-only optimization (cited in reviews as critical gap) | Inventory snapshot integration, reservation system |
    34→| ADR-006 | Baseline/Drift Architecture | Long-term plans cannot recalculate daily; planners need stable reference points | Continuous recalculation (jittery UX, trust erosion) | Published baselines, drift thresholds, dampening |
    35→| ADR-007 | Scenario-Based Simulation | RFQ requires what-if analysis; cannot store in single-reality schema | In-memory only (data loss), Full DB clone (storage bloat) | Event-sourced deltas, materialized comparison views |
    36→| ADR-008 | Infinite Canvas UI (deck.gl) | Semantic zoom from 12-year strategy to daily operations without context switching | Paginated tables (poor UX), Separate views (fragmented) | Progressive enhancement, tabular fallback |
    37→| ADR-009 | Explanation Trace in Optimizer Output | Operators must understand "why" to trust and adopt system recommendations | Black-box output (user rejection, manual overrides) | Factor-based explanations, human-readable descriptions |
    38→| ADR-010 | Audit Trail for All Schedule Changes | 12-year government contract requires full attribution and compliance evidence | Implicit logging only | Immutable history tables, user attribution |

    42→## 1.3 Risk Assessment & Mitigation
    43→
    44→| Risk ID | Risk Description | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy | Owner |
    45→|---------|------------------|-------------|--------|---------------------|-------|
    46→| R-001 | C++ binary build complexity | Medium | High | Containerized build pipeline, pre-built binaries, TypeScript fallback | DevOps |
    47→| R-002 | Team TypeScript focus with C++ component | Low | Medium | C++ contractor with knowledge transfer, comprehensive integration tests | Tech Lead |
    48→| R-003 | Strategic/Tactical solver inconsistency | Medium | High | Constraint aggregation layer, cross-validation tests, unified constraint model | Architect |
    49→| R-004 | Material constraint data quality | High | High | Inventory snapshot validation, fallback to labor-only mode, data quality dashboard | Data Team |
    50→| R-005 | Operator distrust of optimizer | Medium | High | Explanation traces, gradual rollout, manual override capability, training program | Product |
    51→| R-006 | Long-term plan instability (jitter) | Medium | Medium | Baseline snapshots, drift thresholds, dampening in projections | Backend |
    52→| R-007 | deck.gl WebGL performance on low-end devices | Medium | Medium | Progressive enhancement, LOD rendering, tabular fallback | Frontend |
    53→| R-008 | Data migration quality issues | Medium | High | Parallel-run validation, automated reconciliation, rollback procedures | Data Team |
    54→| R-009 | Scenario storage scalability | Low | Medium | Event-sourced deltas vs. full clones, automatic scenario expiration | Backend |
    55→| R-010 | Maximo integration delays | Medium | Medium | CSV interim solution, shadow record system, dedicated integration team | Integration |

   119→## 1.6 Glossary of Terms
   120→
   121→| Term | Definition |
   122→|------|------------|
   123→| PM | Preventive Maintenance (C1, C2, C3, C4, Overhaul) |
   124→| Fixed Parameters | Resource constraints (tracks, man-hours) that bound optimization |
   125→| Run Assignment | Allocation of a specific train to a specific route for a day |
   126→| Mileage Leveling | Balancing cumulative mileage across the fleet to equalize wear |
   127→| Man-hour Leveling | Balancing workforce allocation across time periods |
   128→| Compliance Window | Acceptable date range for scheduling a PM (earliest to latest) |
   129→| Bow Wave Risk | Accumulation of deferred maintenance causing future overload |
   130→| Soft Constraint | Optimization constraint that can be violated with penalty cost |
   131→| Hard Constraint | Constraint that must never be violated (safety, physical limits) |
   132→| Strategic Horizon | Long-term planning window (1-10 years, quarterly granularity) |
   133→| Tactical Horizon | Medium-term planning window (12 weeks, daily granularity) |
   134→| Baseline | Published snapshot of a plan used as reference for drift analysis |
   135→| Drift | Deviation between actual state and baseline projection |
   136→| Scenario | Alternative version of a plan for what-if analysis |
   137→| Explanation Trace | Human-readable justification for optimizer decisions |
   138→| TCO | Total Cost of Ownership (12-year projected costs) |
   139→| Infinite Canvas | Zoomable timeline interface spanning 12 years |
   140→| Semantic Zoom | Data representation changes based on zoom level |
   141→| Bill of Materials | List of parts required for a specific PM type |
   142→| Material Reservation | Soft or hard allocation of inventory to a scheduled PM |
