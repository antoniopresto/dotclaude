    10→## 1.1 System Purpose
    11→
    12→The Metro Fleet Mileage Management System (MMS) is a constraint-based fleet management platform that optimizes train deployments and maintenance scheduling over a 12-year contract period. It replaces manual Excel-based planning with an integrated system providing:
    13→
    14→1. **Daily mileage tracking** with automated ingestion from CSV uploads
    15→2. **Run assignment optimization** balancing mileage across the fleet
    16→3. **Preventive maintenance scheduling** within resource constraints
    17→4. **Strategic planning** for 1-10 year horizons to prevent "bow wave" accumulation
    18→5. **Man-hour leveling** to optimize workforce utilization
    19→6. **Cost forecasting** with TCO projection and variance analysis
    20→7. **Simulation capabilities** for what-if scenario planning
    21→8. **Unified visualization** via an Infinite Canvas interface
    22→9. **Service demand validation** ensuring fleet availability meets operational requirements
    23→10. **Unavailability awareness** integrating corrective maintenance status for schedule validity

    27→## 1.2 Project Scope
    28→
    29→### 1.2.1 In Scope (MMS Manages)
    30→
    31→| Capability | Description |
    32→|------------|-------------|
    33→| Fleet roster management | Trains, cars, components with lifecycle tracking |
    34→| Preventive Maintenance scheduling | C1, C2, C3, C4, Overhaul planning with constraint validation |
    35→| Run assignment optimization | Daily route allocation with mileage balancing |
    36→| Mileage projection and tracking | Route-based utilization profiles, 12-year forecasting |
    37→| Constraint validation | Track capacity, labor, materials, unavailability |
    38→| Maximo integration | Bidirectional sync for PM work orders |
    39→| Multi-year capital planning | Bow-wave detection, cost smoothing |
    40→| Service demand validation | Ensuring minimum fleet availability targets |
    41→
    42→### 1.2.2 In Scope (MMS Must Receive/React To)
    43→
    44→| External Data | Source | Purpose |
    45→|---------------|--------|---------|
    46→| Corrective maintenance status | Maximo webhook/polling | Block unavailable trains from scheduling |
    47→| Inventory snapshots | ERP/Maximo | Validate material constraints |
    48→| Service demand requirements | Operations team | Set minimum availability targets |
    49→
    50→### 1.2.3 Out of Scope (MMS Does Not Execute)
    51→
    52→| Capability | Owner System | MMS Interaction |
    53→|------------|--------------|-----------------|
    54→| Corrective maintenance work orders | Maximo CMMS | Receives status updates only |
    55→| Parts inventory management | ERP/Maximo | Consumes snapshots for constraint validation |
    56→| Operator scheduling | Workforce management | None |
    57→| Real-time GPS tracking | OCC systems | None |
    58→
    59→> **Critical Clarification:** "Out of Scope" means MMS does not *manage* these functions. However, MMS **must receive** corrective maintenance status to prevent scheduling conflicts. Without this visibility, every PM schedule is potentially invalid on the day of execution.

    63→## 1.3 Key Architectural Decisions
    64→
    65→| ID | Decision | Rationale | Alternatives Considered | Risk Mitigation |
    66→|----|----------|-----------|------------------------|-----------------|
    67→| ADR-001 | TypeScript-First Architecture | Team expertise, single language for 98% of codebase, strong typing | Java (enterprise), Python (data science) | Node.js fallback path documented |
    68→| ADR-002 | C++ Native Binary for Tactical Optimization | Container density (20-30 pods/node), <5ms cold start, OR-Tools native performance | WASM solver (limited), Pure TS (10x slower) | TypeScript fallback solver, Docker build pipeline |
    69→| ADR-003 | Dual-Horizon Planning (Strategic + Tactical) | RFQ requires 1-10 year planning; single solver cannot handle both granularities | Single solver (bow wave risk), Manual strategic planning | Constraint aggregation layer ensures consistency |
    70→| ADR-004 | Soft Constraints with Explainability | Operations cannot accept "INFEASIBLE"; must provide actionable least-bad solutions | Hard constraints only (operationally unacceptable) | Fallback greedy solver, explanation trace |
    71→| ADR-005 | Material Constraints in Optimizer | PM scheduling without parts availability is operationally useless | Labor-only optimization (cited in reviews as critical gap) | Inventory snapshot integration, reservation system |
    72→| ADR-006 | Baseline/Drift Architecture | Long-term plans cannot recalculate daily; planners need stable reference points | Continuous recalculation (jittery UX, trust erosion) | Published baselines, drift thresholds, dampening |
    73→| ADR-007 | Scenario-Based Simulation | RFQ requires what-if analysis; cannot store in single-reality schema | In-memory only (data loss), Full DB clone (storage bloat) | Event-sourced deltas, materialized comparison views |
    74→| ADR-008 | Infinite Canvas UI (deck.gl) | Semantic zoom from 12-year strategy to daily operations without context switching | Paginated tables (poor UX), Separate views (fragmented) | Progressive enhancement, tabular fallback |
    75→| ADR-009 | Explanation Trace in Optimizer Output | Operators must understand "why" to trust and adopt system recommendations | Black-box output (user rejection, manual overrides) | Factor-based explanations, human-readable descriptions |
    76→| ADR-010 | Audit Trail for All Schedule Changes | 12-year government contract requires full attribution and compliance evidence | Implicit logging only | Immutable history tables, user attribution |
    77→| **ADR-011** | **Unavailability Event Integration** | PM scheduling requires knowledge of trains blocked by corrective work; without this, schedules are invalid on day of execution | Ignore corrective (operationally invalid) | Maximo webhook integration; manual status update UI as fallback |
    78→| **ADR-012** | **Service Demand as Hard Constraint** | Optimizer must know minimum fleet availability target; cannot validate bow-wave risk without knowing "how many trains do we need?" | Assume 100% availability (unrealistic) | Require service demand input during onboarding; default to 85% fleet availability if unknown |
    79→| **ADR-013** | **Route-Based Utilization Profiles** | Mileage projection accuracy requires route-specific daily rates, not fleet averages; projection error compounds over 12-year horizon | Fleet-wide average (60,000 km error over 12 years) | Route utilization profiles table; manual override per train; explicit flags for unknown routes |
    80→| **ADR-014** | **PM History Assumptions for Cold Start** | Data migration must handle incomplete historical records with explicit confidence levels; system must not crash or produce invalid projections from missing data | Assume complete history (crash risk), Ignore missing (false alerts) | Assumption tracking table; inference rules; confidence scoring; manual review queue |

    84→## 1.4 Risk Assessment & Mitigation
    85→
    86→| Risk ID | Risk Description | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy | Owner |
    87→|---------|------------------|-------------|--------|---------------------|-------|
    88→| R-001 | C++ binary build complexity | Medium | High | Containerized build pipeline, pre-built binaries, TypeScript fallback | DevOps |
    89→| R-002 | Team TypeScript focus with C++ component | Low | Medium | C++ contractor with knowledge transfer, comprehensive integration tests | Tech Lead |
    90→| R-003 | Strategic/Tactical solver inconsistency | Medium | High | Constraint aggregation layer, cross-validation tests, unified constraint model | Architect |
    91→| R-004 | Material constraint data quality | High | High | Inventory snapshot validation, fallback to labor-only mode, data quality dashboard | Data Team |
    92→| R-005 | Operator distrust of optimizer | Medium | High | Explanation traces, gradual rollout, manual override capability, training program | Product |
    93→| R-006 | Long-term plan instability (jitter) | Medium | Medium | Baseline snapshots, drift thresholds, dampening in projections | Backend |
    94→| R-007 | deck.gl WebGL performance on low-end devices | Medium | Medium | Progressive enhancement, LOD rendering, tabular fallback | Frontend |
    95→| R-008 | Data migration quality issues | Medium | High | Parallel-run validation, automated reconciliation, rollback procedures | Data Team |
    96→| R-009 | Scenario storage scalability | Low | Medium | Event-sourced deltas vs. full clones, automatic scenario expiration | Backend |
    97→| R-010 | Maximo integration delays | Medium | Medium | CSV interim solution, shadow record system, dedicated integration team | Integration |
    98→| **R-011** | **No corrective maintenance visibility** | **High** | **Critical** | Unavailability event feed from Maximo; fallback to manual status update UI; alert when train scheduled without availability check | Integration |
    99→| **R-012** | **Service demand undefined** | **High** | **Critical** | Require service demand input during onboarding; default to 85% fleet availability if unknown; prominent warning in UI when using defaults | Product |
   100→| **R-013** | **PM history incomplete at migration** | **High** | **High** | Assumption tracking table (`pm_baseline_assumptions`); inference rules from higher-level PMs; confidence scoring; manual review queue for LOW confidence trains | Data Team |
   101→| **R-014** | **Route utilization unknown** | **Medium** | **High** | Default to fleet-wide average with explicit warning flag; allow manual override per train; route profile editor in admin UI | Product |

   167→## 1.7 Glossary of Terms
   168→
   169→| Term | Definition |
   170→|------|------------|
   171→| PM | Preventive Maintenance (C1, C2, C3, C4, Overhaul) |
   172→| Fixed Parameters | Resource constraints (tracks, man-hours) that bound optimization |
   173→| Run Assignment | Allocation of a specific train to a specific route for a day |
   174→| Mileage Leveling | Balancing cumulative mileage across the fleet to equalize wear |
   175→| Man-hour Leveling | Balancing workforce allocation across time periods |
   176→| Compliance Window | Acceptable date range for scheduling a PM (earliest to latest) |
   177→| Bow Wave Risk | Accumulation of deferred maintenance causing future overload |
   178→| Soft Constraint | Optimization constraint that can be violated with penalty cost |
   179→| Hard Constraint | Constraint that must never be violated (safety, physical limits) |
   180→| Strategic Horizon | Long-term planning window (1-10 years, quarterly granularity) |
   181→| Tactical Horizon | Medium-term planning window (12 weeks, daily granularity) |
   182→| Baseline | Published snapshot of a plan used as reference for drift analysis |
   183→| Drift | Deviation between actual state and baseline projection |
   184→| Scenario | Alternative version of a plan for what-if analysis |
   185→| Explanation Trace | Human-readable justification for optimizer decisions |
   186→| TCO | Total Cost of Ownership (12-year projected costs) |
   187→| Infinite Canvas | Zoomable timeline interface spanning 12 years |
   188→| Semantic Zoom | Data representation changes based on zoom level |
   189→| Bill of Materials | List of parts required for a specific PM type |
   190→| Material Reservation | Soft or hard allocation of inventory to a scheduled PM |
   191→| **Unavailability Event** | A corrective maintenance event, incident, or external factor that removes a train from service (source: Maximo CM work orders, incidents, or manual entry) |
   192→| **Service Demand** | Minimum number of trains required for passenger service by day type (weekday, Saturday, Sunday, holiday) |
   193→| **Utilization Profile** | Expected daily mileage accumulation rate based on route assignment (e.g., NSL = 350 km/day, EWL = 180 km/day) |
   194→| **Cold Start** | Scenario where PM history is incomplete during data migration, requiring estimation with confidence levels |
   195→| **Track Capability** | Specific equipment or features available on a maintenance track (e.g., pit access, crane, lifting jacks) |
   196→| **PM Capability Requirement** | Equipment or track features required to perform a specific PM type |
